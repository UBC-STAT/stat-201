Recap¶

  • We have a population of interest (fishes in BC) and we would like to estimate the population parameter (mean fish length)
  • We take a sample of size 10 fishes and calculate a point estimate (e.g., mean length of the sample)
  • We repeat this process several times to get a sampling distribution of point estimates (e.g., a distribution of mean length)
  • We can quantify the uncertainty of our point estimates by calculating the standard deviation of point estimates (aka standard error!)

Sample size and its relation to standard error¶

Recall that standard error is the standard deviation of point estimates

$$\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$

  • $\sigma_{\hat{p}}$: standard deviation of sample proportions (e.g. proportions of red balls)
  • $\sigma$: standard deviation of population (given)
  • n: sample size
$$\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$

  • $\sigma_{\bar{x}}$: standard deviation of sample mean (e.g., mean fish length, mean GPA)
  • $\sigma$: standard deviation of population (given)
  • n: sample size

As the sample size increases, our standard error decreases
